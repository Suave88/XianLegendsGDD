First 30 Minutes Design

üéØ Onboarding Philosophy

Learn by playing, not reading.
Show, don't tell. Integrate tutorials into story moments.
Never overwhelm‚Äîintroduce one concept at a time.
Players should feel capable, not confused.

üåÖ Phase 1: First 5 Minutes (Awakening)

üé¨ Scene: Ayane's Crystal Awakening

Players start immediately in-game (no title screen tutorial). Ayane awakens in Green Forest, confused and alone. Natural introduction through story.

Teaching Movement

  • Visual prompt: Glowing objective marker shows where to walk
  • Input hint: Small, non-intrusive UI shows WASD/joystick (fades after use)
  • Contextual: "Move toward the light" appears in dialogue, not UI box
  • Immediate feedback: Camera smoothly follows, particles trail behind character
Mobile UX: Virtual joystick appears automatically with pulsing animation. Disappears after first successful movement.

Teaching Interaction

  • Ayane finds a glowing Memory Fragment
  • Prompt appears only when close: "Press [SPACE] to examine"
  • First interaction triggers brief cutscene revealing memory flashback
Goal: Within 5 minutes, players understand movement, interaction, and are emotionally invested in Ayane's mystery.

‚öîÔ∏è Phase 2: Minutes 5-15 (First Encounter)

üé¨ Scene: Meeting Mion & Setsuna

After wandering deeper, Ayane encounters two travelers‚ÄîMion Chen and Setsuna Torres. They're exploring the forest and offer to help. First social bond moment.

Teaching Dialogue & Choices

  • Dialogue wheel: 2-3 response options appear (mobile: large tap targets)
  • No "wrong" answers: All choices lead to friendship (theme reinforcement)
  • Character portraits: Expressive anime art shows emotions clearly
  • Skip option: Hold to fast-forward for returning players

First Combat Tutorial

  • Hostile creature appears‚Äîfirst battle is scripted, cannot lose
  • Grid explanation: "Tap cells to move. Enemies can't reach you from here!"
  • Skill introduction: "Try Mion's 'Strategic Strike' skill (tap icon)"
  • Combo hint: "Setsuna can protect allies‚Äîplace her in front!"
  • Battle won automatically after 2-3 turns, even if player makes mistakes
Mobile UX: Drag-and-drop character placement. Large skill buttons (60px). Grid cells highlight valid moves in green.
Goal: Players understand combat basics, meet the trio, and feel a spark of friendship forming.

üéØ Phase 3: Minutes 15-30 (Tutorial Complete)

üé¨ Scene: Journey to Wunan Village

The trio decides to travel together to Wunan Village. On the way, players practice all core mechanics in real situations.

Combat Refinement

  • Real battle: 3 enemies, player must actually think tactically
  • Celestial Blessing introduced: "Your friendship makes you stronger together!"
  • Guardian Relic teaser: Mion mentions legends of Four Guardians
  • Post-battle rewards: Jade currency, experience points, tutorial on leveling

Exploration & Activities

  • Treasure chest: "Press [E] to open" ‚Üí First consumable items
  • Cooking introduction: Rest at campfire, simple cooking minigame
  • Character bonding: Short dialogue scene showing personalities
  • Map system: "Press [M] to see where we're going"

Base Camp Preview

  • Arrive at Wunan Village‚Äîsafe hub area
  • Uncle Hendrik: Friendly NPC explains village services
  • Save point: "Rest here to save your progress"
  • Shop tutorial: "Spend Jade on supplies for your journey"
  • Quest board: Optional side quests available (not mandatory)
Mobile UX: Bottom navigation bar introduced with all 5 core menu functions. Quick tutorial on swipe gestures.
Goal: By minute 30, players understand ALL core systems and are ready for the full adventure.

üìö Progressive Tutorial System

Advanced Features (Introduced Later)

  • Chapter 2: Guardian Relics unlocked (first Guardian: Qinglong)
  • Chapter 3: Cultivation system explained at Jade Sanctuary
  • Chapter 4: Base Camp customization unlocked
  • Chapter 5: Advanced combat tactics (zone effects, combos)
  • Chapter 6: Full power‚Äîall systems mastered
Design Pattern: Each chapter introduces 1-2 new mechanics naturally through story progression. Never dump everything at once.

üéÆ Tutorial Best Practices

DO's ‚úÖ

  • Show, don't tell: Animate the action, then let players try
  • Contextual prompts: Hints appear only when needed
  • Forgiving first battles: Early fights cannot be lost
  • Immediate rewards: Every tutorial action gives positive feedback
  • Skip option: For returning players or experts
  • Integrated into story: No "training room" disconnected from narrative

DON'T's ‚ùå

  • No text walls: Never show 3+ paragraphs of instruction
  • No pop-up spam: One tutorial prompt at a time
  • No "read the manual": Everything learnable through play
  • No punishment for learning: Mistakes are encouraged early on
  • No mandatory grinding: First 30 minutes is story-focused

üì± Mobile-Specific Tutorial

Touch Controls Introduction

  • Gesture tutorial: Animated hand shows swipe directions
  • Virtual joystick: Pulsing circle guides first movement
  • Tap vs Hold: Clear visual feedback for different inputs
  • Two-finger gestures: Explained only when camera rotation needed
  • Button customization: "Uncomfortable? Adjust in settings!" prompt
Adaptive UI: Detects screen size and adjusts button placement automatically. Suggests left-handed mode if available.

‚ôø Accessibility in Tutorials

Inclusive Onboarding

  • Difficulty options: "Story Mode" for relaxed play, "Tactical Mode" for challenge
  • Text-to-speech: All tutorial prompts can be read aloud
  • Adjustable speed: Combat speed can be slowed for learning
  • Color-blind support: Grid cells use icons + colors
  • Repeated prompts: Tutorial hints re-appear if player seems stuck
  • Auto-confirm option: Reduces required button presses
